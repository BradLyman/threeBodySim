There are two things to focus on when we examine the simulation. First, we want to understand what we are simulating (e.g. the physics). And second we want to understand how we go about solving the equations that the physics provides us with.

The Physics

A three body gravitation system is just any system where there are 3 objects which are acting on each other gravitationally. Three body systems are interesting because they seem like they should be simple, but in reality they form a complex set of dynamical systems. Because of this complexity we choose to use a three body system where one of the bodies is so small that its influence on the overall system is insignificant. Choosing to model the system this way is usefull because we are left with what is essentially a two body system (which is well understood). From here forwards we will refer to our two large bodies as "planets" and the third insignificant body as a "particle".

Two body gravitational systems have analytic solutions, so we can model the two planets without worrying about numeric differential equation solvers. However, the particle is a different story...
